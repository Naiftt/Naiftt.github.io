About Me

I am Naif Alkhunaizi, a graduate with a Master's degree from Mohamed bin Zayed University of Artificial Intelligence, which is the world's first AI university specializing in Machine Learning. Currently, I am working as a research assistant, where I continue to develop my skills and knowledge in the field of AI and Machine Learning. During my graduate studies, I had the opportunity to work on various research projects and gained experience in many different aspects of machine learning. One of my most significant accomplishments was developing an aggregation rule to suppress malicious attacks in Federated Learning, which was published in Miccai, one of the world's most prestigious medical imaging conferences. My research interests are primarily focused on Federated Learning, Privacy-Preserving Machine Learning, and Computer Vision. I am passionate about using AI to solve real-world problems and exploring the potential of AI in various fields, including healthcare. As a research assistant, I continue to work on projects related to privacy and security in Machine Learning, including Federated Learning and Differential Privacy. Additionally, I am involved in developing new methods to improve model robustness and fairness. In my free time, I enjoy playing sports, such as volleyball and table tennis, and I am always on the lookout for new technologies and innovations that can help improve people's lives.
