About Me

My name is Naif Alkhunaizi. I'm currently a master's student at Mohamed bin Zayed University of Artificial Intelligence, the world's first AI university majoring in Machine Learning. I have done my bachelor's degree at Arizona State University, majoring in Electrical Engineering. I lived in the US for five years and went through many memorable moments, and met people from different worldwide views. I was a member of an engineering fraternity where we hosted many events on campus, and I got to build relationships with fellow students. Also, I was part of a campus club where we had a weekly meeting to discuss and embrace our differences and get to know each other's culture.
During my last semester, I was an undergraduate teaching assistant for Random Signals Analysis class, where I had the chance to help students deepen their understanding. I'm fascinated with probability theory, and it was one of the reasons I wanted to continue my graduate studies in Machine Learning and develop intelligent models.
I started my studies in Jan 2021 in the UAE at Mohamad Bin Zayed University of Artificial Intelligence and gained many skills in class and outside class. I was one of the finalists of GITEX 2021, where we came up with a way to reduce car traffic during rush hours. My research interest is mainly in privacy for Federated Learning and currently working with Transformers to improve Federated Learning setting to accelerate convergence and enhance security. Moreover, I worked on several projects such as using GANs to generate animated images, utilize causal discovery and inference to prove that smoking is a leading factor of lung cancer, and demonstrate that specialist intervention can help children with impaired cognitive skills. In my free time, I like to play ping pong, volleyball, and chess. Lastly, I find new technologies fascinating, and I'm aiming to build new products in the future that can make our life easier.
